stock photos

Images: Mizuno K

Studios would love to treat actors like stock photo models.

Typically, an actor will sign up for a job - a movie or show - and provide a performance for that specific production, or more if they make more instalments or seasons.

Stock models will be photographed for stock photos and sign away their likeness so that those photos can be reused over and over again for other things.

What if you could employ an actor when they're cheaper to hire, get a 3D scan of their body, and then exploit them if they become a star?

All you need is their face.

Image: Tima Miroshnichenko

background actors

Image: Emre Akyol

When a production needs a shot of a crowd of people, they must obtain release forms from everyone on screen. As a result, it’s often simpler to hire background actors/extras, which means paying more people. To an executive looking to reduce costs, it’s a problem needing a solution.

So what if everyone was replaced by CGI replicas? A company could purchase a person’s likeness rights and use it forever.
